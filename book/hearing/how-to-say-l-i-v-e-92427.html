How to Say 'l-i-v-e'

Hello! This week on Ask a Teacher, we answer a question from Lola.

Question

Hello,


I'm Lola from Madrid.

My question is about the pronunciation of the word "live". Sometimes the pronunciation is "liv" and others "laiv." The same with the word "lives." Why?

Thank you very much.

Answer

Thank you for writing, Lola. This is such a good question!

"Live" is an example of a heteronym, or words that have the same spelling but different pronunciations and meanings.

There are nearly 100 heteronyms in English. But let's get to your question about the word spelled l-i-v-e today.

It can be either an adjective, an adverb, or a verb.

When it is used as an adjective or adverb, we use the /a&#618;/ sound, like "laiv."

I love live music.

Are you watching the game live?

When it is used as a verb, we use the /&#</I>618;/ sound, like "liv."</p> <p><em>Where do you <u>live</u>?</em></p> <p><em>We <u>live</u> just outside the city.</em></p> <p>As for the word spelled l-i-v-e-s, it can be either a plural noun (from the singular "life") or third person singular verb.</p> <p>When it is a plural noun, we use the /a<I>&#</I>618;/ sound:</p> <p><em>Cats have nine <u>lives</u>.</em></p> <p>When it is used as third person singular, we use the /<I>&#</I>618;/ sound.</p> <p><em>She <u>lives</u> with her mother</em>.</p> <p>So, in order to know which pronunciation to use for l-i-v-e and -s, you want to look carefully at how it is used in a sentence.</p> <p>Please let us know if this explanation has helped you, Lola.</p> <p>Do you have a question about American English?
