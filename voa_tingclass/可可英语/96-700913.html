In flooded agricultural fields near the Tennessee River, tens of thousands of birds called sandhill cranes search for food such as berries, corn, seeds and insects.
在田纳西河附近被洪水淹没的农田里，数以万计的鸟类，被称为沙丘鹤，正在寻找食物，如浆果、玉米、种子和昆虫。
The cranes come to the Wheeler National Wildlife Refuge Center in northern Alabama during the winter.
鹤群在冬季来到阿拉巴马州北部的惠勒国家野生动物保护区中心。
The yearly movement, or migration, of sandhill cranes brings many birdwatchers who want to see the tall birds up close.
每年，沙丘鹤的迁徙活动都会吸引众多观鸟者，他们希望近距离观察这些高大的鸟类。
The birdwatchers also come to see the rare and endangered whooping cranes that migrate in much smaller numbers to Wheeler.
观鸟者们来到这里，也是为了观赏稀有的、濒危的美洲鹤，迁徙到惠勒美洲鹤数量要少得多。
Many other birds can be seen, including geese, ducks, bald eagles, kestrels and hawks.
还能看到许多其他鸟类，包括鹅、鸭子、秃鹰、红隼和鹰。
Park ranger David Young described the area as "a birder's paradise," meaning it is a perfect place for birdwatchers.
公园管理员大卫·杨称这片区域是“观鸟者的天堂”，这意味着，这里是观鸟爱好者的理想之地。
The cranes fly from the Great Lakes, an area far to the north, to Alabama each year.
每年，鹤群从遥远的北方大湖区飞往阿拉巴马州。
The sandhill cranes started migrating to Wheeler for the winter in the mid-1990s.
沙丘鹤在20世纪90年代中期开始迁徙到惠勒过冬。
Their numbers increased by a lot in the mid-2000s, Young said.
他们的数量在2000年代中期大幅增加，杨说。
Last year, the migratory sandhill population reached a new record of 30,000 cranes.
去年，迁徙的沙丘鹤种群数量达到了新的纪录，共有3万只。
The refuge has become a popular place for birdwatchers who bring hand-held telescopes called binoculars.
这个保护区已成为观鸟者的热门地点，他们带来了手持望远镜，称为双筒望远镜。
Birdwatchers can even get closer to the 1.2-meter-tall cranes as visitors stay hidden behind photography blinds and a two-level observation center.
观鸟者可以更接近这些1.2米高的鹤，游客们则隐藏在摄影掩体和两层高的观察中心后面。
A yearly Festival of the Cranes in January brings more attention and visitors.
每年一月的鹤类节吸引了更多的关注和游客。
The cranes put on a fun show, dancing on their thin legs, moving their wings and putting their long beaks in the air to make different sounds.
鹤群们上演了一场有趣的表演，它们用细长的腿跳舞，挥动着翅膀，将长长的喙伸向空中，发出不同的声音。
Diana Maybury-Sharp regularly comes to Wheeler with friends to hike and look at birds.
戴安娜·梅伯里-夏普经常和朋友一起来惠勒，徒步旅行，观赏鸟类。
"It's pretty extraordinary. I've seen them in other parts of the country where there were just a few," Maybury-Sharp said.
“这里超级独特。我在国内的其他地方见过它们，但只有少数几只，”梅伯里-夏普说道。
"They're not vocal like they are here. There's so many here that it's an unusual experience."
“它们不像这里的鹤这样爱鸣叫。这里有很多鹤，让人感觉非比寻常。”
The whooping cranes are part of the eastern migratory population that comes from the north-central state of Wisconsin.
美洲鹤属于东部迁徙种群，它们来自中北部的威斯康星州。
Young said they started coming to Alabama in 2004. Their population is between 12 and 20 each winter.
杨说，他们从2004年开始来到阿拉巴马州。每年冬季，它们的数量介于12到20之间。
Whooping cranes nearly died out in the early 1900s because of hunting and farming.
美洲鹤在20世纪初因狩猎和农业活动几乎灭绝。
The International Crane Foundation says recovery and reintroduction efforts have slowly increased their wild and captive populations to more than 800.
国际鹤类基金会表示，恢复和重新引入的努力已经让野生和圈养的鹤群数量缓慢增加到800多只。
The whooping cranes are sometimes hard to see among the sandhills, but their height and white feathers help them stand out.
在沙丘鹤群中，有时很难发现美洲鹤，但它们的身高和白色羽毛使它们显得格外突出。
Young said the migration patterns of the cranes might change depending on the climate of both their nesting places to the north and their winter homes in the south.
杨表示，鹤类的迁徙模式可能会发生变化，这取决于它们北方筑巢地和南方越冬地的气候。
Rob Broeren brought along his camera and a long telephoto lens and found a good place to work on his wildlife photography skills.
罗布·布罗伦带上了他的相机以及一个长焦镜头，找了个合适的地方，去钻研他的野生动物摄影技巧。
He comes to the refuge about twice a month each year to photograph the birds and ducks.
他每年，每月大概会来这个保护区两次，拍摄鸟类和鸭子。
"You just need to be patient and wait for them to do something interesting and make their calls," Broeren said.
“你只需要耐心等待，等它们做出一些有趣的事情，等它们鸣叫，”布罗伦说。
"You get that cool shot and that's a good day."
“只要拍到那张酷炫的照片，那就是美好的一天。”
The sandhill cranes will start leaving the refuge in mid-February.
沙丘鹤将在二月中旬开始离开保护区。
Young said he notices a lot more activity when the birds are showing they are ready to travel back to their nesting grounds.
杨说，他注意到，当鸟类准备好返回筑巢地时，活动量会显著增加。
"It's usually on a day when we have some sort of a south wind," Young said.
“通常是在有南风的日子，”杨说。
"They're smart and they will ride the wind back to their breeding grounds whenever it's most convenient for them."
“它们很聪明，会在最方便的时候，乘风返回它们的繁殖地。”
