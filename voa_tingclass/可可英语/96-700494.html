California officials have declared a state of emergency over the spread of bird flu.
禽流感正在蔓延，加州官员宣布进入紧急状态。
It is affecting milk cows in that state and causing illnesses in some people in other U.S. states.
它影响到了加州的奶牛，并导致美国其他州的一些人患病。
The avian influenza virus, or bird flu, has spread among wild birds, farm birds and many mammal species.
禽流感病毒已经在野生鸟类、农场鸟类和许多哺乳动物物种中传播。
The virus is known as Type A H5N1. It was identified for the first time in U.S. milk cows in March.
这种病毒被称为A型H5N1。今年3月，它首次在美国的奶牛身上被发现。
Since then, bird flu has been found in about one third of U.S. states.
从那时起，美国大约三分之一的州都发现了禽流感。
The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is following the number of cases and is reporting the latest details on its website.
美国疾病控制和预防中心(CDC)正在跟踪病例数量，并在其网站上报告最新细节。
Most of the illnesses have been mild.
大多数病例都比较轻微。
Health officials said that one person in Louisiana was hospitalized with the nation's first known severe illness caused by the virus.
卫生官员说，路易斯安那州有一人因感染该病毒而入院治疗，成为全国首例严重病例。
California Governor Gavin Newsom said he declared the state of emergency so state workers could take faster action.
加利福尼亚州州长加文·纽瑟姆表示，他宣布进入紧急状态，以便州工作人员能够更快地采取行动。
California officials have been testing milk tanks. They said they have found the virus in hundreds of herds.
加州官员一直在检测牛奶罐。他们说已经在数百个牛群中发现了这种病毒。
The virus was recently found in Southern California dairy farms.
最近在南加州的奶牛场发现了这种病毒。
It was found in the state's Central Valley in August.
8月份在该州的中央山谷发现了它。
Officials with the CDC repeated that the public has a low risk of getting the virus.
疾控中心的官员重申，公众感染病毒的风险很低。
There are no reports of person-to-person infection and no signs that the virus has changed to spread easily among humans.
目前还没有人传人的报告，也没有迹象表明病毒已经改变，能很容易地在人类之间传播。
Flu experts say it is too soon to know how the situation will change.
流感专家表示，目前还不清楚情况会如何改变。
But some experts are worried because flu viruses are mutating all the time and small genetic changes could take place.
但一些专家担心，因为流感病毒一直在变异，可能会发生微小的基因突变。
Health officials said the first known case of severe illness in the U.S. was found in a patient in Louisiana.
卫生官员说，美国已知的第一例严重病例是在路易斯安那州的一名患者身上发现的。
The person is older than 65 and has other medical problems.
这名患者年龄超过65岁，伴有其他健康问题。
Officials said the person showed severe breathing problems after being near a group of sick birds.
官员们说，这名男子在接近一群生病的鸟后出现了严重的呼吸问题。
The CDC said that the Louisiana case is the first confirmed U.S. infection connected with backyard birds, or birds kept near a home.
疾控中心表示，路易斯安那州的这个病例是美国首例确认与后院鸟类（即在家中饲养的鸟类）有关的感染病例。
Almost all the earlier infections in the U.S. have been in farmworkers dealing with infected dairy cattle or birds.
在美国，几乎所有早期感染病例都发生在与被感染的奶牛或禽类打交道的农场工人身上。
In two cases - an adult in Missouri and a child in California - health officials have not learned how they became infected.
在两个病例中--密苏里州的一名成年人和加利福尼亚州的一名儿童--卫生官员尚未确定他们是如何感染的。
Angela Rasmussen is a virus expert at the University of Saskatchewan in Canada.
安吉拉·拉斯穆森是加拿大萨斯喀彻温省大学的病毒专家。
She said as more people become infected, more severe illnesses will be reported.
她说，随着更多的人被感染，将会报告更严重的疾病。
Rasmussen said, "I assume that every H5N1 virus has the potential to be very severe and deadly."
拉斯穆森说：“我假设每种H5N1病毒都可能非常严重和致命。”
The World Health Organization says on its website that from 2003 to November 2024 about 142 people died from the H5N1 virus around the world.
世界卫生组织在其网站上表示，从2003年到2024年11月，全球约有142人死于H5N1病毒。
Most of the deaths were in Southeast Asia.
大多数死亡病例发生在东南亚。
People who have contact with dairy cows, commercial poultry or with backyard birds are at higher risk than other people.
接触奶牛、商业家禽或后院鸟类的人比其他人风险更高。
CDC and other experts said those people should use breathing, eye, and hand protection, especially if the animals appear ill or if they die.
疾控中心和其他专家表示，这些人应该使用呼吸、眼睛和手部保护，特别是在有动物生病或死亡的情况下。
The CDC said in July that it would buy $5 million in vaccines for farmworkers to protect against seasonal flu.
疾控中心在7月份表示，将为农场工人购买500万美元的疫苗，以预防季节性流感。
If someone becomes infected with two flu types at the same time, it might permit the bird flu virus to mutate and to become more dangerous.
如果有人同时感染了两种流感病毒，可能会让禽流感病毒发生变异，变得更加危险。
The H5N1 virus can also be spread in raw, or unpasteurized, milk.
H5N1病毒也可以在生牛奶或未经巴氏灭菌的牛奶中传播。
The U.S. Food and Drug Administration says pasteurized milk is safe to drink because the heat treatment kills the virus.
美国食品和药物管理局说，巴氏杀菌牛奶可以安全饮用，因为热处理能杀死病毒。
But high levels of the virus have been found in unpasteurized milk.
但在未经巴氏灭菌的牛奶中曾发现高浓度的病毒。
Health officials urge people to avoid drinking raw milk.
卫生官员敦促人们不要饮用生奶。
It can spread other germs in addition to bird flu.
除了禽流感，它还会传播其他细菌。
The U.S. Agriculture Department has increased its testing of raw milk across the country to identify and contain avian flu.
美国农业部在全国范围内增加了对生奶的检测，以识别和控制禽流感。
Federal officials ordered milk testing which began this month in 13 states.
联邦官员下令本月开始在13个州进行牛奶检测。
