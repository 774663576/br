A group of sheep on farmland in the American state of Texas work together to complete a job: eating the grass growing beneath hundreds of solar panels.
美国德克萨斯州农田里的一群羊在一起完成一项工作：把几百块太阳能电池板下面的草吃掉。
Sheep have become a symbol of a growing solar industry as large-scale solar farms appear across the United States.
美国各地出现了大规模太阳能发电场，绵羊也成为了日益增长的太阳能产业的象征。
In Milam County, Texas, SB Energy operates the fifth-largest solar project in the country.
SB Energy在德克萨斯州的米拉姆县运营着美国的第五大太阳能项目。
It can produce 900 megawatts of power across 1,618 hectares.
在1618公顷的土地上，它可以生产900兆瓦的电力。
So, how does the company deal with all the grass that grows on such a large piece of land?
那么，在这么大一块土地上长出的青草，公司该如何处理呢？
It deploys about 3,000 sheep that keep the grass cover low.
它安排了大约3000只绵羊，尽量降低青草覆盖率。
Sheep, unlike devices such as lawnmowers, can fit between small spaces and work in all kinds of weather conditions.
与割草机等设备不同，绵羊可以在狭小的空间中工作，并且能适应各种天气条件。
The work of the sheep, and sometimes other animals, is called solar grazing.
绵羊的工作，有时还有其他动物的工作，被称为太阳能放牧。
It is considered part of the larger idea of agrivoltaics --- the use of land for both solar energy production and agriculture.
它被认为是更宏大的农业光伏理念的一部分--利用土地生产太阳能，同时也兼顾农业。
The U.S. Department of Agriculture suggests that solar grazing may help the environment in several ways.
美国农业部表示，太阳能放牧对环境的保护作用体现在几个方面。
These include making grassy areas safer for wild animals, like nesting ground birds, and by reducing fuel use and air pollution.
包括让草地上的野生动物更安全，比如在地面筑巢的鸟类，并且可以减少燃料消耗和空气污染。
Agrivoltaics is on the rise with more than 60 solar grazing projects in the U.S., the National Renewable Energy Laboratory says.
美国国家可再生能源实验室说，美国有60多个太阳能放牧项目，农业光伏正在兴起。
The American Solar Grazing Association says 27 states take part in solar grazing.
美国太阳能放牧协会表示，有27个州参与了太阳能放牧。
Putting the animals to work on solar fields also provides some help to the sheep and wool market, which has struggled in recent years.
让动物在太阳能发电场地工作也为近年来举步维艰的绵羊和羊毛市场提供了一些帮助。
The number of sheep and lambs in Texas fell to 655,000 in January 2024, a 4 percent drop from the year before.
2024年1月，德克萨斯州的绵羊和羔羊数量下降到了65.5万只，比前一年下降了4%。
That information comes from the most recent data from the U.S. Department of Agriculture.
这是来自美国农业部的最新数据。
Because solar fields use sunny, flat land that is often good for animals to eat grass on, the power plants have been used in cooperation with farmers rather than against them.
太阳能场地选取的都是阳光充足、平坦的土地，很适合动物在上面吃草，所以发电厂一直与农民合作利用土地，两者之间并不是敌对关系。
Sheep farmer JR Howard accidentally found himself in the middle of Texas' move to clean energy.
牧羊人JR·霍华德意外地发现自己参与到了德克萨斯州向清洁能源转变的过程中。
In 2021, he and his family began working with solar farms.
2021年，他和家人开始与太阳能发电场合作。
Howard's sheep ate the grass on these large areas of land.
霍华德的羊在这些大面积的土地上吃草。
What was once a small business has turned into a larger operation with more than 8,000 sheep and 26 employees.
曾经的小企业已经变成了拥有8000多只羊和26名员工的大型企业。
"Just the growth has been kind of crazy for us," said Howard, who named his company Texas Solar Sheep. "It's been great for me and my family."
霍华德把自己的公司命名为德克萨斯太阳能羊，他说：“光是羊群数量的增长就已经有点疯狂了。我和家人都觉得很棒。”
Some agriculture experts say Howard's success shows how solar farms have become a big help for some ranchers.
一些农业专家说，霍华德的成功表明，太阳能发电场已经成为一些牧场主的一大助力。
Reid Redden is a sheep farmer and solar vegetation manager in San Angelo, Texas.
里德·雷登是德克萨斯州圣安吉洛的一名牧羊人和太阳能植被经理。
Redden said a successful sheep business requires agricultural land that has become increasingly hard to find.
雷登说，牧羊业的成功需要农业土地，而后者越来越稀缺。
"Solar grazing is probably the biggest opportunity that the sheep industry had in the United States in several generations," Redden said.
“太阳能放牧可能是美国绵羊业几代人以来最大的机会，”雷登说。
Redden suggested that people in the rural communities near where he raises sheep have been very positive about solar grazing.
雷登表示，在他养羊的地方附近的农村社区，人们一直对太阳能放牧持非常积极的态度。
Agrivoltaics itself is not new.
光伏农业这个概念本身并不新鲜。
Solar farms use a lot of land and require a lot of space that could be used for food production.
太阳能发电场需要大量土地，而这些土地本可以用来生产粮食。
Agrivoltaics helps with the problem by permitting the two industries to coexist, whether growing food or caring for livestock.
光伏农业让这两个行业可以共存，不管是种植食物还是饲养牲畜，从而解决了这一问题。
There is a lot still unknown about the full effects of solar grazing, said Nuria Gomez-Casanovas of Texas A&M University.
德克萨斯农工大学的努里亚·戈麦斯-卡萨诺瓦斯说，关于太阳能放牧的全部影响，还有很多未知之处。
Not enough studies have been done to know the long-term environmental effects, such as how good the soil will be for future agriculture.
还没有做足够的研究来了解它产生的长期环境影响，比如土壤对未来农业的好处有多大。
However, Gomez-Casanovas suspects solar grazing may improve sheep productivity.
然而，戈麦斯-卡萨诺瓦斯怀疑太阳能放牧可能会提高绵羊的生产力。
"We really have more questions than answers," Gomez-Casanovas said.
戈麦斯-卡萨诺瓦斯说：“我们的问题其实多于答案。”
