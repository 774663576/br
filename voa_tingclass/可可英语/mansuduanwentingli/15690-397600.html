He has a big window in his bedroom.
他的卧室有一个大窗户。
His desk is in his bedroom.
卧室里有一张桌子。
His desk is next to the big window.
桌子旁边是窗户。
He sits at his desk.
他坐在桌子上。
He looks east.
他向东看。
He looks east toward the East River.
他向东看看见了东河。
The East River is about six blocks away.
东河大约有6个街区远。
But he cannot see the East River.
但是，他看不见东河。
Buildings block his view.
建筑挡住了他的视线。
Buildings in New York City are like trees in a forest.
纽约城的建筑就像是密林中的树木。
They are everywhere.
它们无处不在。
They are beautiful.
它们很美丽。
They are all different.
各有不同。
He can see about 13 different buildings.
他能够看到13种不同的建筑。
The farthest building is almost three blocks away.
最远的一栋有将近3个街区。
Most of the buildings are about 12 stories tall.
绝大多数建筑都有12层。
Most of them are light brown or dirty white.
绝大多数都是浅棕色或是污白色。
But the tallest one is completely red.
但是，最高的那一栋是纯红色。
White smoke comes out of its top.
白烟从建筑顶端飘出。
It has no windows on its west side.
西面没有窗户。
The west wall is red bricks.
东墙满是红砖。
Maybe red bricks are cheaper than glass windows.
或许，红砖要比玻璃窗更便宜。
Maybe it's a prison.
这或许是监狱。
